# 15 · Multimodal — VQVAE & Diffusion Transformers

How does a Transformer eat images, audio, or video when its native input is a sequence of integers? Two answers, both based on the same idea: **discretize continuous signals into tokens, then run a Transformer.**

## VQ-VAE — turning pixels into tokens

A VQ-VAE consists of an encoder $E$, a learned codebook $\{e_k\}_{k=1}^{K}$, and a decoder $D$. The encoder maps an image patch to a continuous vector $z_e$, which is then **snapped to the nearest codebook entry**:

$$
k^* = \arg\min_k \|z_e(x) - e_k\|_2, \qquad z_q = e_{k^*}
$$

The decoder reconstructs $\hat x = D(z_q)$. The full loss has three terms:

$$
\mathcal{L} = \underbrace{\|x - D(z_q)\|^2}_{\text{reconstruction}}
+ \underbrace{\|\text{sg}[z_e] - e\|^2}_{\text{codebook}}
+ \underbrace{\beta\,\|z_e - \text{sg}[e]\|^2}_{\text{commitment}}
$$

where $\text{sg}[\cdot]$ is the stop-gradient. After training, an image becomes a 32×32 grid of integer codes — exactly the kind of thing a Transformer loves.

## Diffusion Transformers (DiT)

Instead of discretizing, **diffusion** generates images by iteratively denoising Gaussian noise. The model learns to predict the noise $\epsilon$ added at step $t$:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{simple}} = \mathbb{E}_{x_0,\,\epsilon,\,t}\!\left[\bigl\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t, t, c)\bigr\|^2\right], \qquad x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1 - \bar\alpha_t}\,\epsilon
$$

DiT replaces the U-Net backbone with a **plain Transformer over image patches** — the same architecture as a language model, just operating on noisy patch embeddings instead of token IDs. This is how Sora, Stable Diffusion 3, and Flux work.
