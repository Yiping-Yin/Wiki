# 11 · KV-Cache — Why Inference Is Fast

A naive Transformer recomputes attention over the entire context for every new token. The **KV-cache** stores the per-layer keys and values from previous steps so that generating token $t+1$ only requires computing one new query against $t+1$ cached entries.

## The redundancy

For a prefix of length $T$, generating one new token without caching costs:

$$
\mathcal{O}\bigl(L \cdot (T+1)^2 \cdot d\bigr)
$$

With a KV-cache it drops to:

$$
\mathcal{O}\bigl(L \cdot (T+1) \cdot d\bigr)
$$

— linear instead of quadratic in $T$. For long contexts this is the difference between "usable" and "unusable."

## Memory cost

$$
\text{KV memory} = 2 \cdot L \cdot T \cdot n_{\text{heads}} \cdot d_{\text{head}} \cdot \text{bytes per element}
$$

For Llama-2-7B at 4096 ctx in fp16: $2 \cdot 32 \cdot 4096 \cdot 32 \cdot 128 \cdot 2 \approx 2$ GB — **per request**. This is why batched serving is hard and why GQA, MQA, and PagedAttention exist.

## Minimal implementation

```python
class CausalSelfAttention(nn.Module):
    def forward(self, x, kv_cache=None):
        B, T, C = x.shape
        q, k, v = self.c_attn(x).split(C, dim=2)
        # reshape to (B, n_head, T, d_head)
        q = q.view(B, T, self.n_head, -1).transpose(1, 2)
        k = k.view(B, T, self.n_head, -1).transpose(1, 2)
        v = v.view(B, T, self.n_head, -1).transpose(1, 2)
        if kv_cache is not None:
            past_k, past_v = kv_cache
            k = torch.cat([past_k, k], dim=2)
            v = torch.cat([past_v, v], dim=2)
        new_cache = (k, v)
        y = F.scaled_dot_product_attention(q, k, v, is_causal=(kv_cache is None))
        return self.c_proj(y.transpose(1, 2).reshape(B, T, C)), new_cache
```

<Callout type="warn">
The KV-cache is the **single biggest reason long-context LLMs are expensive**. Tricks like Grouped-Query Attention (Llama-2) and Multi-Query Attention (PaLM) shrink it by 8–32×.
</Callout>
