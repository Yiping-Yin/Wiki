# 10 · State of GPT (Karpathy @ Microsoft Build)

Karpathy's ~40-minute talk laying out the **modern LLM training pipeline** in one diagram: pretraining → SFT → reward modeling → RLHF.

<YouTube id="bZQun8Y4L2A" title="State of GPT — Andrej Karpathy" />

## The four stages

<Mermaid chart={`flowchart LR
  A[Internet text<br/>~10TB] --> B[Pretraining<br/>base model]
  B --> C[High-quality<br/>prompt/response<br/>pairs]
  C --> D[SFT model]
  D --> E[Comparison data<br/>A is better than B]
  E --> F[Reward model]
  F --> G[RLHF / PPO / DPO]
  D --> G
  G --> H[Assistant model]`} />

| Stage | Data | Algorithm | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pretraining | trillions of tokens of internet text | autoregressive next-token prediction | base model |
| SFT | ~10k–100k high-quality (prompt, response) pairs | same loss as pretraining, smaller LR | SFT model |
| Reward modeling | comparison data: pairs ranked by humans | Bradley–Terry log-likelihood | reward model $r_\phi$ |
| RLHF | prompts from a distribution | PPO (or DPO) against $r_\phi$ + KL to SFT | assistant model |

## The RLHF objective

$$
\max_{\pi_\theta}\; \mathbb{E}_{x \sim D,\, y \sim \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid x)}\!\bigl[r_\phi(x, y)\bigr] - \beta\,\mathrm{KL}\!\bigl(\pi_\theta\,\|\,\pi_{\text{ref}}\bigr)
$$

The KL term is the leash: it stops the policy from drifting too far from the SFT model and gaming the reward.

<Callout type="tip">
Karpathy's takeaway: **RLHF doesn't make models smarter, it makes them helpful**. Capability comes from pretraining; alignment comes from the later stages.
</Callout>
