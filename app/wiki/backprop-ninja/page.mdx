# 5 · Becoming a Backprop Ninja

Disable `loss.backward()` and walk every gradient by hand: cross-entropy, softmax, matmul, BatchNorm, tanh, embedding lookup. The point isn't masochism — it's that after this you'll **never again** be confused about why some gradient is wrong.

<YouTube id="q8SA3rM6ckI" title="Building makemore Part 4: Becoming a Backprop Ninja" />

## The fused softmax + cross-entropy gradient

$$
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial z_i} = p_i - \mathbf{1}[i = y]
$$

That's it. The whole horrible chain through softmax and log collapses into "predicted probabilities minus one-hot target."

## Matmul backward

$$
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial A} = \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial Y}\, B^\top, \qquad
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial B} = A^\top\, \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial Y}
$$

## BatchNorm backward (the scary one)

$$
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x_i} = \frac{1}{N\sigma}\!\left(N\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \hat{x}_i} - \sum_j \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \hat{x}_j} - \hat{x}_i \sum_j \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \hat{x}_j}\hat{x}_j\right)
$$

## Verify against PyTorch

```python
import torch
logits = torch.randn(8, 27, requires_grad=True)
y = torch.randint(0, 27, (8,))
probs = logits.softmax(1)
loss = -probs[range(8), y].log().mean()

# manual gradient
dlogits = probs.clone()
dlogits[range(8), y] -= 1
dlogits /= 8

loss.backward()
print(torch.allclose(dlogits, logits.grad))  # → True
```

<Callout type="tip">
Karpathy's framing: forward broadcasting becomes backward summation, and forward summation becomes backward broadcasting. Once that clicks, manual backprop is mechanical.
</Callout>
